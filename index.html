Frequently Asked Questions about EMDR Therapy

EMDR is Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing, a form of treatment used to help people with traumatic memories. It is a form of psychotherapy that enables people to heal from the symptoms and emotional distress that are the result of disturbing life experiences.

Everything from car accidents and phobias to the loss of a loved one and natural disasters. There are over 25 scientific studies that support EMDR as a treatment for depression, nightmares, PTSD, anxiety, phobias, and traumatic events.

EMDR is done in 1 of 3 ways. All of these ways use bilateral stimulation. All that means is we are causing both sides of the brain to become active so that all of the information stored in the brain can be fully processed. Think of when you walk or run. You are using both sides of your body, usually alternating between right and left. Have you ever noticed that your mind gets clear and you can think “better” or more clearly when you run or go for a walk? This is what bilateral stimulation does to the brain, helps it process information. We are recreating this process using one of three methods.

One is by using eye movement where the therapist uses their fingers and the client follows them back and forth while engaging in therapy. Second is by using what are known as “tappers” which vibrate back and forth while being held in a client’s hand. No, this is not shocking you and I will let you feel the “tappers” so you can choose whether or not you would like to use them. The third way is through the use of headphones where there is a sound going back and forth between them.

Are you or someone you are involved with interested in kink or bondage/discipline/dominance/sadomasochism (BDSM)? I love guiding folx through negotiating, exploring, or beginning to get involved with kink and BDSM in their relationship(s).

I find there can be alot of shame and/or stigma associated with kink and BDSM. There might have been times when you were afraid to mention your interests to a therapist out of fear that the therapist would view this interest as a problem. It can be difficult to talk about this with a therapist who is not informed. I provide room for discovery and negotiation of expectations for folx for themselves or in partnerships in a therapeutic setting.

I help clients in the following ways:
  • Negotiating contracts
  • Working on sexual shame and guilt
  • Being able to bring up and share fantasies with your partner(s)
  • Defining roles and expectations and how this benefits your relationship(s)
  • Expressing your love language and getting needs met
  • Setting boundaries
  • Expanding on your present sex life or your changing sexuality with your partner(s)
  • Exploration of interests between partners
  • Increasing or developing trust within relationships
  • Creating a sexual health road map for yourself and what you feel sexual health looks like
  • Establishing aftercare routines that are beneficial for everyone involved
  • Psychoeducation on kink and BDSM and how it can be integrated into relationship(s)
