EMDR Intensive

Why did I come here? Why is this taking so long? This is what you think as you walk away from your weekly meeting with your therapist. You know weekly appointments help and that change is happening but...it's...so...slow. You do not have the time to wait months or years for things to improve. You thought that once you took the jump and got started then it would be different. It took so much just to start therapy and now you feel you just don’t have the time to get as deep as you need to.

You deserve to have the time and space to go where you need to go. To look at the things that have been put away, tucked into a corner and on your to do list for a long time. With an EMDR intensive you have the time and space to unpack that corner and decide what to keep, what to toss, and what needs attention here and now.

Weekly therapy sessions make a difference for you but you want something that fits your needs and your time frame. You can do an EMDR intensive as an adjunct therapy to weekly/biweekly sessions or an intensive on it’s own. When you come in for an intensive there is no one else scheduled. You have the therapist, office, and day(s) all to focus on yourself with no rush and no pressure to get it all done in 50-55 minutes.

Doing an intensive EMDR session can be financially advantageous. Even compared to other trauma therapy doing an intensive format can decrease time in therapy and cost as you are not spending time a) checking in when arriving to the session, b) addressing day to day concerns, c) working extensively on skills that you may or may not need after therapy, and d) the wrap up as you head home. With those things out of the way you can hunker down and get through what you want or need to to go back into the world feeling rejuvenated and restored.

An EMDR intensive includes:

  • A free 30 minute video telehealth consultation
  • A 90 minute initial intake
  • A plan and goals that fit your needs and your time
  • Coordinated follow up care with your existing therapist
  • After doing an intensive EMDR session you will feel relief and have a sense of being able to return to more regular day to day living. I use metrics to help you see the improvement in your thoughts and symptoms so that it isn’t just me telling you that things have shifted or changed.

If you still have questions please reach out to me at info@lagniappeemdr.com and I can schedule a consultation and send you any additional information that you would be interested in.

Bipolar Disorder

You wake up and before your foot even hits the floor you wonder if today will be one of THOSE days. A day where everything is too big, too loud, too much and nothing is simple. It’s so confusing. Some days it feels like time is just slow and other days where you feel like you cannot catch up no matter how hard you try. You feel directionless about what needs to change so that you can keep up. Do you ever wonder where you stop and the bipolar disorder starts? What if you just got told you have bipolar disorder? Your world, the one you thought was normal, has now been revealed to you to not be like everyone else’s world. The things you love about yourself, your passion, your energy, your spontaneity, and even your sexuality have all been explained as “abnormal” or “part of being manic”. You may not even have the language right now to know what that means or how to tell your doctor or those you care about how the things you do are normal for you.

Bipolar disorder is complicated. Getting a plan in place doesn’t have to be. Having someone who knows how to take your struggle and turn it into something you can plan for and even take advantage of at times rather than being to your detriment is attainable. Yes, you have a disease but that doesn't mean you can’t figure out how to work with it and keep going.You want someone who will take the time to explain how this disease works and is showing up in your life. Someone who will help you to know when it is mania and not just enthusiasm for a new project or potential passion. Someone to check in with to see if things are askew.

That’s where I come in. Through working with you, your doctor, gathering data, and finding patterns I can help you become more confident in how to manage your day to day. I can help you find a plan that works for the times when it feels like you just can’t take any more of the roller coaster ride. Together we can create a plan that will show you how to handle mood management, have more confidence in yourself, and be able to identify when and how to use your coping skills effectively.

Stop wondering if this is as good as it gets. You don't have to continue to cycle between shame and regret around your illness and how it affects you. You could continue to go back and forth until you are exhausted, but you don’t have to. Reach out and schedule your free 30 minute consultation and see how I can help. Let's get you to where every day doesn’t feel like a struggle.

First responders, medical, mental health

Serving as a first responder is unique in many ways. Whether you work from a squad car, fire truck, ambulance, hospital, or back at the station, you make life-altering decisions every day. Your decisions could change not only your life but others as well. Now, more than ever, the pressure is on for you to do your job by the book and it can be a strain on your life.

What happens when you go home at night and the day is over? You know there will always be more to do. Thoughts of another call or another emergency create a never-ending chain of stressful thoughts and sleepless nights. Are you able to separate work and home? When you first start out it is exciting work and you get to help people, which makes you proud and happy to serve. Then you start to notice changes. Often, your friends or family notice before you do. Sometimes they are the ones who tell you that you’ve changed, or that you seem a little off. It’s the smaller things that you too start to notice. You’re irritable, you're quick to anger, or you can never seem to relax, so you distance yourself from the people who are important to you. Recurring dreams of past incidents come to mind at the worst moments. You can’t pinpoint why or when this started, but now all you want is to make it stop.

That’s where I come in. You will work with someone who understands the uniqueness of serving as a first responder, someone you don’t have to explain everything to or edit yourself with. You can talk to someone who has the professional experience to help you figure out a plan to get back to tackling anything that comes your way, whether on the job or at home. Don’t keep bottling this up, hoping it gets better on its own. Know that it’s okay to reach out for professional support.
